The Coffee Belt

The Coffee Belt refers to the band of tropical regions around the world where coffee grows best. This zone lies between the Tropic of Cancer and the Tropic of Capricorn, covering countries with warm climates, rich soil, and just the right mix of sunlight and rainfall. Because coffee plants are sensitive and need stable temperatures, the Coffee Belt provides the perfect environment for producing high quality beans.

Starbucks works closely with farmers across the Coffee Belt through its Coffee and Farmer Equity (C.A.F.E.) Practices. This ensures that the beans they source are ethically grown, high quality, and produced in ways that support both the environment and the livelihood of farmers. The unique climates and traditions in each region also help Starbucks create a wide variety of blends for different tastes.

Three Main Coffee Regions

Latin America

Known for bright, clean flavors with a hint of nuttiness and cocoa. Latin American coffee is often light and easy to drink, which is why Starbucks uses many beans from this region for its classic blends.

Africa

Famous for vibrant, fruity, and floral coffees. African beans usually have more unique flavor notes like berries or citrus, which makes them popular for single-origin Starbucks Reserve coffees.

Asia/Pacific

Known for bold, full-bodied coffees with earthy or spicy taste. Many Starbucks dark roasts and “stronger-tasting” blends use beans from this region.
